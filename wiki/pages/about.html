What is Creative Non-Fiction?

Creative Non-Fiction (CNF) is a genre that merges literary techniques with factual storytelling. Rather than present reality as a series of raw facts, CNF writers borrow techniques of fiction writing— description, anecdote, scene construction, characterization, and dialogue—to tell dynamic and compelling true stories. The crucial distinction between creative nonfiction and fiction is that nonfiction purports to tell the truth with very little embellishment, while fiction claims to be “made up.” Creative nonfiction also draws from poetic approaches to language, including imagery, metaphor, tone, and shifts in point of view and perspective. (Syracuse University Project Advance)

SUPA Creative Non-Fiction at ASIJ

Syracuse University Project Advance (SUPA) Creative Nonfiction is a year-long English course at the American School in Japan that immerses students in the art of storytelling through real-life experiences. Students read and analyze a diverse range of creative nonfiction texts while crafting their own essays, experimenting with voice, style, form, and research to deepen their writing. In the Publication Unit, each student contributes a piece to the collective anthology Komorebi, showcasing their unique perspective and growth as writers.
